ABOUT ME

In elementary school, I used to vividly dream about people trying to murder me. Fortunately, I have always been a lucid dreamer, so I usually escaped death by forcing my dream-self to chew gum (if you’ve seen the movie Inception, gum-chewing was my “kick” to wake myself up. I like to think I’m Leonardo DiCaprio).

 

The nightmares eventually subsided, but my dreams (both night and day) remain as intense and elaborate as ever. Thanks to my knack for controlling these reveries, my dreams are packed with plots and pictures rivaling that of novels and films. Sleep cycles are spent constructing and exploring hundreds of imaginary worlds, embarking on countless adventures. Flying is a particularly favorite dream pastime of mine.

 

It is the same control and craftsmanship that I proceed to bring to the real world. My head isn't always stuck in the clouds! A dedicated, organized, and passionate student, I am, however, a storyteller above all else. Writing novels, drafting scripts, performing on stage and behind cameras, I’ve experimented with every possible storytelling medium in an effort to bring to reality the fantastical pieces of my imagination.

 

I am currently studying marketing and accounting at the University of Maryland, College Park. Despite being a business major, I aspire to surround myself with fellow storytellers, and am eager to work in the entertainment or publishing industries.

Besides daydreaming the day away and lucid-dreaming the nights, I avidly partake in many different aspects of the arts. I especially enjoy performing arts, with my favorite roles being Puck from A Midsummer Night's Dream and Elizabeth Proctor in The Crucible. I am currently playing the Grim Reaper in a silent film entitled, Grimmy, for the University of Maryland's Filmmaking Club.

 

In high school I was fortunate enough to perform for numerous guests, including President Obama, multiple times at Christmas in Washington events and Kennedy Center Honors. Singing alongside the likes of Jennifer Hudson, Hugh Jackman, and Diana Ross is an experience I will never forget.

 

I also enjoy creative writing, having completed two novels and am currently working on a third. It involves a lot of space travel.

 

On the visual arts side, I've always liked traditional mediums (I'm a massive doodler), but have recently become interested in lettering, typography, design, and illustration, especially in digital forms. Vaguely related is my strange hobby of making small cardboard boxes out of larger cardboard boxes.

 

Outside of the arts, I love the outdoors, especially skiing, and have been itching to hang glide for years. I imagine the sensation to be similar to how it feels to fly in my dreams.
